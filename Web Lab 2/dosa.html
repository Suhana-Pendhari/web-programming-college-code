Authentic South Indian Dosa Recipe

Discover the crispy, golden delight of homemade Dosa, a popular South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter. This recipe makes about 10-12 dosas and takes around 8-10 hours (including fermentation time). Perfect for breakfast or any meal!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice (preferably parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • Water (as needed for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately. Soak them in water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Drain the water and grind the soaked ingredients into a smooth batter using a blender or wet grinder, adding water gradually to achieve a pancake-like consistency.
  3. Add salt to the batter and mix well. Cover and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours (or overnight).
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa tawa over medium heat. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it thinly in a circular motion.
  5. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and cook until the bottom is golden and crispy (about 2-3 minutes).
  6. Flip if desired, or fold and serve hot with chutney, sambar, or potato filling.

Tips

  • For Masala Dosa, prepare a potato filling by saut√©ing boiled potatoes with onions, spices, and curry leaves.
  • The batter should be slightly sour after fermentation for the best flavor.
  • Store leftover batter in the fridge for up to 2 days.
